Smoked Beef Brisket

Description

This smoked beef brisket is a masterpiece of backyard barbecue. Cooked low and slow, the meat becomes tender and juicy with a rich, smoky flavor that’s perfect for rugged meat lovers.

It’s a time-honored tradition that requires patience and care, but the mouthwatering results are well worth the wait.

Ingredients

  • 1 (5 pound) beef brisket
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • Wood chips for smoking (hickory or mesquite recommended)

Steps

  1. Preheat smoker to 225°F (107°C).
  2. Combine paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  3. Rub the spice mixture all over the brisket.
  4. Place brisket in the smoker and insert a meat thermometer.
  5. Smoke until internal temperature reaches 195–205°F (90–96°C), about 1.5 hours per pound.
  6. Wrap brisket in foil during last couple hours if preferred for moisture.
  7. Let rest for 30 minutes before slicing against the grain and serving.
